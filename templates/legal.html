Privacy Policy

Last update: 02-09-2025

Data Collection: PostFlow collects the following user data: username, name, and profile picture, if the user grants access to PostFlow to post via API. This information is displayed when the user clicks the Authorize button on the Integrations page in the modal corresponding to one of the platforms supported by PostFlow for scheduling posts.

Data Use and Processing: The collected data is saved to a database via SSL and used on the Integrations page to display the username and profile picture next to the integration authorized by the user. This helps users identify which pages/profiles are managed by PostFlow. Currently, only one account is supported for each platform (e.g., one Facebook public page, one Instagram business account, one X account, one LinkedIn account, one TikTok business account). Login with Google is used to authenticate users via OAuth2. All data is used to enable PostFlow to post via API on behalf of the user and to identify users in case they require support.

Data Storage and Security: User data is stored in a cloud-based SQL database. HTTPS/SSL is used to secure data during transfer. The application packages and operating system are regularly updated to ensure the app remains secure from potential threats. While no system is entirely immune to breaches, we continuously monitor the application's health and take action as needed.

User Rights: Users can access their data within the app and delete it or revoke access to external APIs on the Integration page. Users can also revoke access on the respective third-party apps. For example, users can forbid access to PostFlow by adjusting their Google settings. Users can request their data to be sent to them via email by contacting alinclimente@softgata.com.

Data Sharing: User data is not shared with third parties.

Last update: 02-09-2025

User Responsibilities and Prohibited Activities: Users must not intentionally cause outages or attempt to hack the app to access other users' data. Users must adhere to the terms of service of the platforms they use to publish their posts. Users must use the app for its intended purpose and should not attempt to disrupt its functioning. Users are encouraged to report any issues with the app by opening an issue on GitHub at ClimenteA/social-media-posts-scheduler or by emailing alinclimente@softgata.com. Reports should include detailed steps to reproduce the error. Users must not post content that is not compliant with terms of service and community standards of the platforms they are posting on.

However, PostFlow is not responsible if posts are not published due to unrefreshed access tokens or downtime of external APIs. In cases where external APIs change and cause issues with PostFlow, efforts will be made to restore functionality promptly. Since users have a trial period to assess the app, PostFlow does not offer refunds. Users experiencing outages may receive free usage at the discretion of PostFlow's owner(s). Under no circumstances will PostFlow be liable for monetary reimbursement.

Cookie Policy

Last update: 02-09-2025

Cookie Usage: Cookies are stored in the browser to provide secure authentication for users and to protect forms with CSRF tokens. Local storage is used to store user preferences, such as cookie consent, to enhance the user experience.

Managing Cookie Preferences: Since cookies are essential for authentication, deleting them from the browser will require users to re-authenticate for each request made to PostFlow.

Compliance with Digital Services Act (DSA)

Last update: 02-09-2025

While our app is primarily designed for scheduling posts on third-party platforms and currently serves a limited user base of under 10,000 users within the EU, we are committed to ensuring compliance with the Digital Services Act (DSA) through several key practices:

Content Monitoring: We manually monitor user-generated content to prevent misuse of our platform. Although our user base is small, we take proactive steps to address any illegal or harmful activities by banning users who violate our terms of service.

Transparency: Our app is open-source, with the code available on GitHub. This allows users and the community to understand how our app operates, including our algorithms and data usage practices.

Data Privacy and Security: We prioritize the security and privacy of our users' data. Regular updates and continuous investigations are conducted to identify and mitigate potential security risks.

User Responsibility: Users are responsible for the content they schedule. We provide clear guidelines and terms of service to inform users of their responsibilities.

Risk Management: We continuously assess and update our app to address potential issues, ensuring safe and secure operation for all users.

As we grow, we will continue to evaluate and enhance our compliance measures to align with evolving requirements of the DSA and other relevant regulations.
